Xteke inspectors check the surface with a colorimeter and optical comparators for optimal aesthetic performance, based on the standard that requires that the surface of a new part matches exactly the custom color in a full-color spectrum.

Xteke inspectors control the dimensions of a part by using a wide array of measurement gauges during the whole production process; some of the tools used are a micrometer, thread gauge, digital calipers, and height gauge.

A concrete angle degree is the core of sharp-angled parts, like welding parts. All of the parts with an exclusive angle degree will be examined and approved by a bevel protractor and square tester to ensure they match the sketch perfectly.

For the purpose of producing the part with a custom decorative finish upon request, Xteke machinists will carefully measure the thickness of the surface finish of aluminum parts by using the layer thickness gauge.

The post-processing surface finish modifies the appearance and the function of your machined parts.

For the purpose of providing the precise part with a specific roughness degree or other decorative post-processing surface finish, inspectors will test the surface of the finished aluminum part by using the surface rough meter.
